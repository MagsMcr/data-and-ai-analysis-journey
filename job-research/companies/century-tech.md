## Part 1: Company Overview

CENTURY Tech is a London-based EdTech company that builds an AI-powered adaptive learning platform for schools, colleges, tutoring businesses, and home learners. It was founded by Priya Lakhani OBE, whose core insight was that artificial intelligence combined with neuroscience and learning science could create truly personalised educational experiences — adapting to each student rather than forcing students to adapt to rigid structures. The platform covers English, maths, and science from KS1 through to post-16 and GCSE resit level, and has expanded internationally with clients in Pakistan, Croatia, and through a partnership with Cognita, one of the world's largest school groups.

The two problems CENTURY sets out to solve are that many students do not meet required standards, and that educators spend up to 65% of their time on administrative tasks such as marking and data tracking — time that is not spent improving the quality of instruction. The platform addresses both sides simultaneously: students get personalised learning pathways, and teachers get automated data and reporting. CENTURY claims teachers save up to six hours per week by using the platform.

The company has a home learning product (used by parents directly, from £7.99/month), a school/MAT product, an FE college product, and a tutoring business product — meaning its data spans multiple user types and learning contexts. This is relevant because a data analyst at CENTURY would potentially be working across all of these product lines.

**Key people:**
- **Priya Lakhani OBE** — Founder & CEO (barrister-turned-entrepreneur, prominent voice in UK EdTech)
- Academic partnerships with UCL (machine learning and pedagogy) and University of Leeds (diagnostic and cognitive research)

## Part 2: The Data Function

CENTURY is fundamentally a data business. Every click, every score, every interaction on the platform is recorded, feeding into AI algorithms that learn how each individual student learns and plot the most effective route through learning material. This means the data infrastructure is core to the product — not a support function bolted on afterwards.

**What the data does inside the product:**
- Generates personalised learning pathways for each student in real time
- Identifies knowledge gaps and flags students who need intervention
- Provides teachers with performance analytics dashboards showing student progress at individual and class level
- Automates progress reports and routine assessments, removing manual data entry from teachers

**What this means for a data analyst role there:**
The data function at CENTURY would likely sit at the intersection of product analytics (how is the platform performing?), learning analytics (are students actually improving?), and commercial analytics (which schools are engaging, renewing, expanding?). Someone in a data analyst role would probably be working with large behavioural datasets — interaction logs, assessment scores, engagement patterns — and translating them into insights for product, pedagogy, and sales teams.

Given that CENTURY serves teachers, school leaders, parents, and students, the "communicating to non-technical audiences" skill that appears in almost every target job posting would be directly relevant here. A teacher dashboard is only useful if the data behind it is interpreted and presented clearly.

**Support site structure (from direct access):**
The support site is organised by user type — Teacher, Leadership, Student, Parent/Guardian. This tells you something about how CENTURY thinks about its data: different stakeholders need different views of the same underlying information. That layered reporting structure is a clue to what an analyst might build and maintain.

## Part 4: Personal Angle

**Genuine strengths for this company specifically:**

You have used the product. This is not a trivial advantage — you can speak to the user experience from the perspective of both the parent and (implicitly) the student. You understand what the teacher dashboard is trying to do, what the personalised pathway feels like in practice, and what questions a parent would want answered from the data. Most candidates applying to CENTURY have not actually used the platform and will be speaking abstractly about EdTech.

Your background in business development and event management also maps well to a scale-up context. CENTURY is growing — they need people who can work across functions, communicate clearly, and move quickly. That's not a developer profile; it's closer to yours.

The target role for you here in 12–18 months is likely a **learning analytics or product analytics role** rather than a pure data engineering position. The question to research further: does CENTURY have a dedicated data/insights team, or do analysts sit within product? LinkedIn profiles of current employees may help answer this.

**One honest flag:** CENTURY is a relatively small company (scale-up, not enterprise). That means the data team is probably small too, which could mean broader scope and faster learning — or it could mean limited progression structure. Worth probing in any future conversation with them.
