## Market Scan: March 2026

### EdTech (Century Tech, Lexplore, school-facing roles)

**Overall picture:** Active hiring market. Roles range from data engineer/analyst at schools and MATs through to product analyst at EdTech platforms. Strong emphasis on student outcome data, operational analytics, and communicating insights to non-technical stakeholders (teachers, SLT).

**Typical role titles seen:**
- Data Analyst
- Data Engineer (AI & Analytics)
- Product Analyst
- Learning Analytics Specialist

**Consistent technical requirements across postings:**
- Python (most common)
- SQL (near-universal)
- Visualisation tools — Tableau, Power BI, Looker
- Dashboard building
- Ability to work with student / LMS data

**Soft skills consistently mentioned:**
- Communicating complex data to non-technical audiences
- Cross-functional collaboration (product, curriculum, ops teams)
- Translating business questions into analytical approaches

**Notable posting:** Berkhamsted Schools Group — Data Engineer (AI & Predictive Analytics), £36–60k, closing March 2026. Wanted data architecture experience, analytics solutions, insights for students, staff and operations. Good example of the school/MAT employer type.

---

### Academic Publishing & Assessment (Cambridge UP&A, Bloomsbury, Pearson type)

**Overall picture:** Smaller but high-quality talent market. Cambridge UP&A is actively hiring across multiple data functions. Roles tend to blend research methodology with data skills — more R and statistical literacy than pure engineering. Strong mission-alignment expected.

**Typical role titles seen:**
- Data Analyst (Assessment)
- Senior Data Analyst
- Monitoring & Evaluation Analyst
- Academic Data Analyst
- Automation Analyst

**Consistent technical requirements:**
- Python, R, or SQL (often "or" — flexibility on language)
- Statistical analysis and research methods
- Power BI or similar for dashboards
- Data visualisation for varied audiences
- Some roles: SPSS, SAS, NVivo (more academic/research end)

**Soft skills consistently mentioned:**
- Communicating findings to technical AND non-technical stakeholders
- Research methodology awareness
- Clear, precise documentation
- Collaborative team working

**Notable posting type:** Cambridge UP&A Monitoring & Evaluation Analyst — asked for quantitative research methods, dashboard maintenance, visualisations for diverse audiences, experience with education datasets. Good target role profile.

**Key insight:** The "communicating to non-technical audiences" requirement appears in every single posting in this sector. This is a genuine strength to emphasise.

---

### Education Nonprofits & Charities (National Literacy Trust, Education Endowment Foundation type)

**Overall picture:** Smaller volume of postings but growing. Charities increasingly treating data as strategic asset, not just compliance reporting. Budget constraints mean analysts often wear multiple hats. Mission alignment expected and can substitute somewhat for technical depth.

**Typical role titles seen:**
- Data Analyst
- Research Analyst
- Impact Analyst
- CRM & Data Manager (often combined)

**Consistent technical requirements:**
- Excel (still dominant in this sector)
- SQL increasingly required
- Python / R desirable but not always essential
- Power BI or Tableau for dashboards
- CRM systems (Salesforce, Dynamics) often mentioned

**Soft skills consistently mentioned:**
- Impact measurement and programme evaluation
- Combining data and narrative (reports, briefings)
- Working with non-technical colleagues
- Flexible, multi-functional approach

**Key insight:** Technical bar is slightly lower than commercial EdTech, but impact measurement framing is important. "What does this data tell us about what's working?" is the core question in this sector.

## Skills Gap Analysis (as of March 2026)

Mapping market requirements against current learning plan status:

| Skill | Market importance | Current status | Plan coverage | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Python / pandas | High | Building (Week 3) | Yes — Weeks 1–4 | ✅ On track |
| SQL | Very high | Not started | Yes — Week 5 | ⚠️ Don't delay |
| Data visualisation (matplotlib/seaborn) | High | Not started | Yes — Week 6 | ⚠️ Coming up |
| Tableau or Power BI | High | Not in plan | Not currently planned | 🔴 Flag for later |
| R | Medium | Installed, basic | Yes — Weeks 13–14 | ✅ In plan |
| Statistical methods | Medium | Not started | Yes — Week 7 | ✅ In plan |
| Communication to non-technical audiences | Very high | Strong existing skill | Ongoing via portfolio READMEs | ✅ Strength |
| Domain knowledge (education/publishing) | Medium-high | Building via research | Ongoing | ✅ In progress |
| Impact measurement framing | Medium (charities) | Not yet | Not explicitly in plan | 📌 Worth adding |

**Key takeaway from this scan:**
- SQL in Week 5 is well-timed — do not let it slip
- Tableau or Power BI is not in the current 18-week plan but appears frequently enough to be worth adding a light introduction, possibly Week 8 or Week 14
- The "communicating to non-technical audiences" strength should be made explicit in every portfolio project README — it's a genuine differentiator
